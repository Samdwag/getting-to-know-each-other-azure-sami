Greatest innovations

First Working Programmable Computer: Z3 (1941)

Konrad Zuse created the Z3 which was an electromagnetic computer for which program and data were stored on external punched tapes.

Learn more about the Z3

First Popular High Level Lanugage: Fortran

Fortran was released in 1957 and was created by a team led by John Backus at IBM. It had been in use for over half a century in scientific and engineering area such as computational fluid dynamics, finite element analysis, computational chemistry, and computational physics.

Learn more about Fortran!

First Mobile Phone: DynaTAC 8000X

The first mobile cell phone was the DynaTAC 8000X. It had to be charged for 10 hours for 30 minutes of talk time. It was originally designed by Motorola engineer, Martin Cooper. Although the phone was invented in 1973, it was still a prototype and would take 10 years and $100 million in development costs for it to become to the general public.
